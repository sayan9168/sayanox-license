# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## 1. Is the Sayanox License OSI-approved?
No, the Sayanox License is a custom license. It is not currently approved by the Open Source Initiative (OSI). If you need a strictly OSI-approved license, consider using MIT, Apache 2.0, or GPL.

## 2. Can I use this license for my commercial project?
Yes, you can use it for commercial projects. However, because it is a custom license, it is highly recommended to have a legal professional review it to ensure it meets your specific business needs.

## 3. How do I give credit/attribution?
You must retain the copyright notice and the license text in all copies or substantial portions of the software. See `HOW_TO_APPLY.md` for exact instructions.

## 4. Can I modify this license for my own project?
Yes, but if you modify it, you **must** change the name of the license (e.g., "My Custom License") to avoid confusion with the official Sayanox License.

## 5. What happens if there is a legal dispute?
The license is provided "AS IS", without warranty. Any legal disputes should be resolved according to the jurisdiction specified in the license terms.
## 6. Can I use the Sayanox License alongside MIT or GPL in my project?
Yes, the Sayanox License is designed to be permissive. 
- If your project has multiple components, you can license Component A under MIT and Component B under Sayanox. 
- However, if you are combining code, ensure that the terms of both licenses do not conflict. For complex multi-license projects, consult a legal professional.
